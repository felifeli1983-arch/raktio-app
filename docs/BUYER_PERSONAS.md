# BUYER_PERSONAS.md

## Purpose

This document defines the core buyer personas, secondary buyer personas, and market-facing use cases for Raktio.

Raktio has broad theoretical applicability, but broad applicability is not the same as strong go-to-market focus.  
This file clarifies:
- who is most likely to understand and buy the product first
- what pain each persona has
- what they use Raktio for
- what message resonates with them
- how personas differ in willingness to pay and product fit

---

## Core market rule

**Raktio is useful for many people, but it should not be positioned as “for everyone” in its primary go-to-market message.**

The strongest early strategy is:
- broad underlying applicability
- focused messaging and entry wedges

This is especially important because Raktio is a new category-like product.

---

## Product category lens

Raktio is best understood as a:

- social perception simulation platform
- synthetic audience testing system
- reaction intelligence product
- pre-launch scenario exploration engine

It is not primarily:
- a chatbot
- a market forecast oracle
- a social media scheduling tool
- a generic sentiment dashboard
- a traditional survey platform

---

## Buyer persona tiers

Buyer personas should be grouped into three tiers:

### Tier 1 — Core launch personas
Most strategically important and easiest to justify early product value

### Tier 2 — Strong secondary personas
Good fit, strong use cases, potentially high value, but not necessarily the first messaging wedge

### Tier 3 — Speculative / selective personas
Interesting long-term or niche use cases, but not ideal to lead with

---

# Tier 1 — Core launch personas

## 1. Brand / Marketing Manager

### Who they are
People responsible for:
- campaigns
- messaging
- product positioning
- launch assets
- audience resonance
- avoiding waste and backlash

### What they need
- test campaign reactions before launch
- find objections before spending budget
- compare variants
- understand how different audiences react
- identify message risk

### Why Raktio fits
They already think in terms of:
- audience
- message
- perception
- reaction
- testing
- performance

Raktio feels like an evolution of tools they already understand.

### Typical use cases
- test ad concepts
- test campaign slogan
- test brand message
- test launch narrative
- compare 2–3 variants before going live

### Key value message
**“Test how your audience may react before you publish or spend.”**

### Willingness to pay
High if:
- the output feels credible
- the report feels actionable
- the compare workflow saves real campaign waste

---

## 2. Founder / Product Marketer / Startup Operator

### Who they are
People making early decisions about:
- startup idea validation
- feature rollout
- pricing
- messaging
- landing pages
- GTM positioning

### What they need
- validate assumptions
- detect objections early
- see if the idea is misunderstood
- compare positioning angles
- stress-test demand narratives

### Why Raktio fits
They are already using LLMs to think through ideas, but they only get one intelligent voice back.

Raktio offers:
- multi-agent reaction simulation
- audience-based variation
- compare loops
- deeper blind spot discovery

### Typical use cases
- validate startup idea
- test pricing reaction
- test landing message
- compare feature framing
- see what audience segments reject the concept

### Key value message
**“Go beyond one AI opinion. Simulate audience reaction before committing.”**

### Willingness to pay
Moderate to high, depending on:
- perceived credibility
- ability to run several iterations affordably
- usefulness versus just asking Claude or ChatGPT

---

## 3. PR / Crisis / Communications Team

### Who they are
People responsible for:
- press statements
- crisis responses
- internal or external communications
- issue escalation handling
- reputation-sensitive messaging

### What they need
- stress-test messaging before release
- identify backlash triggers
- detect language that escalates risk
- compare calmer vs riskier framing
- understand how different publics respond

### Why Raktio fits
This is one of the strongest product fits because:
- the cost of communication failure is high
- scenario simulation is intuitive here
- patient zero / amplifier / faction analysis is highly valuable

### Typical use cases
- simulate response to a public statement
- test apology wording
- compare press release variants
- identify which segment/geography reacts badly

### Key value message
**“See where your message breaks before the public does.”**

### Willingness to pay
Very high when the communication is high stakes.

- a stronger story to bring to clients

- presentation material

- compare creative directions
- test audience reaction to concepts

**“Bring your client a simulated audience reaction before the real launch.”**

High, especially if Raktio improves pitch quality or upsell value.

Teams responsible for:

- test how a post lands

# Tier 2 — Strong secondary personas

## 6. Media / Entertainment / Publishing

### Who they are
- studios
- producers
- publishers
- editorial teams
- showrunners
- narrative/content strategists

### What they need
- test plot twists
- test titles/headlines
- test reactions to character choices
- test tone or controversy risk
- compare audience reactions before release

### Why Raktio fits
Narrative reactions are social, emotional, and segmented—perfect for this product.

### Typical use cases
- test finale reaction
- compare article headlines
- test controversial story arc
- simulate fandom/community response

### Value message
**“Simulate public reaction before a narrative choice becomes irreversible.”**

### Willingness to pay
High in premium projects, moderate in general publishing.

- employee communications

### What they need
- simulate employee/public reaction to policy

### Value message

### Willingness to pay
Moderate to high in enterprise contexts.

## 8. Legal Strategy / Mock Audience Use Cases

### Who they are
- law firms
- litigation strategy teams
- high-stakes communication/legal framing advisors

### What they need
- explore how a narrative is perceived
- test public/jury-like reactions to framing
- identify weak points in messaging
- test language and persuasion angles

### Important caution
Raktio should not be sold as:
- a truth-finding legal oracle
- a judicial decision engine
- evidence of factual correctness

It is useful for:
- reaction simulation
- narrative testing
- perception analysis
- objection discovery

### Typical use cases
- mock audience reaction to a legal narrative
- compare framing strategies
- identify persuasive weak points

### Value message
**“Stress-test your narrative and discover objections before the room does.”**

### Willingness to pay
Potentially high in the right use cases.

## Positioning guidance

### What not to say first
Do not lead with:
- “for everyone”
- “simulate anything”
- “millions of agents”
- “AI predicts the future”

### What to lead with
Lead with:
- testing audience reaction before launch
- discovering objections and blind spots
- comparing versions before spending money
- seeing how segments/geographies/platforms react differently

This makes the product understandable.

---

## Credibility guidance

Raktio should be sold as:
- scenario exploration
- reaction simulation
- blind-spot discovery
- synthetic audience testing

Not as:
- certainty engine
- future oracle
- objective proof of market truth

This is important for both trust and product longevity.

---

## Final rule for Claude

**Use buyer personas to shape messaging, defaults, examples, onboarding, and commercial positioning. Raktio has broad applicability, but its strongest launch personas are marketing, founders/product marketing, PR/crisis, agencies, and content/social teams. Build for broad applicability, but message with strategic focus.**
